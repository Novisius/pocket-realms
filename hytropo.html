Hytropo

The realm is named Hytrop(i/a/o). (Hi-trope-(ee/ah/oh))

Each denizen calls it either Hytropi, Hytropa, or Hytropo; based upon the gender they identify with. -i for non-binary or fluid, -a for feminine, and -o for masculine.

-e (ey) is another suffix, used for neutral gender.
-u (oo) is another suffix used for unknown genders or to be formal.
Attaching a -y to a suffix turns it plural.

Hytrope and Hytropu are uncommon forms of the realm’s name, but possible to encounter.

Within the language of the denizens of the realm, all nouns used these suffixes. To use a car as an example, if no one was driving it, the word would end in -e, as it is a simple object without a will or soul, but if a man was driving it, it would end in -o and a woman driving it, -a.

Names utilize this method as well, however, it is based on the person who owns the name to choose a suffix, unlike with objects.

Terrain

The center of the realm is a series of evershifting cracks surrounding the liquid metal realmic core. Occasional breaches to the metal allow slivers to escape or scant growths of Moxite/Etherium/Crystals (MEC) to grow out.

These cracks widen out into wide caverns, each one relatively diverse. Some have their own flora and fauna, others have lakes and streams, a few even have enough consistent MEC to serve as light sources. Most of the realm's moderates have found homes and refuge within these caverns, in particular, the ones with consistent light.

These caverns are not simply in the ground, they are within the horizon dominating pillar of black stone that spans many kilometers across the center of the realm. Tunnels weave through this structure in a mess that few even dare to consider to map out, leaving only a few well known, established, and predictably safe pathways to and from the caverns and the outside.

Outside of this spire lies a realm of darkness. The clouds black as they choke out the sun, the air near poison to those unacclimated, a caustic and toxic fog clings to the barren ground like brine in the ocean. Above it flies the sanctuaries, both arcane and technological, circling the spire in a slow dance. The Edgebound and Versebound humans of this realm had decended into a rather violent form of tribalism, centuries ago, which resulted in a catastrophic war. What remains of the realm is the aftermath of that conflict, grand magics against devastating technologies. Believe it or not, the environment has recovered by leaps and bounds since then. Before they could destroy everything, they came to an agreement, they would not see each other ever again. The Edgebound humans were relugated to one side of the spire, the Versebound humans to the other. They both crafted sanctuaries above the devastation, but at some point, they moved. Best theories maintain that it was due to a crash that caused a sanctuary to shift into line of sight of the other side, which prompted them to shift, which prompted their neighbors to shift, which prompted the other side to shift. So now, they circle the spire slowly, perhaps one kilometer an hour if that. During the war, the moderates had more or less taken the innards of the spire, and that area is officially considered to be 'neutral ground'. Which why it is now where most realmic trade occurs.

Further still from this area, the lands have begun to heal, in a generous sense. Waters are contaminated with oils, chemicals, and poisons. The air would still be hard to breath for you or I, leading to a significantly shorter and arguably painful, life. The ground is rocky and irradiated, not enough to kill now, but enough to induce cancers in the next decade or so. What little plantlife has been able to claw their way back to a notable presence is venomous and poisonous, not actually on purpose, but as a byproduct of the air, water, and soil. The plants themselves are usually grasses or lichen of some variety, depending on the location.

Edgebound Humans

The edgebound humans of the realm live in small ships, only large enough to house a small family unit and a contingent of mage-hunters. The contingent can range from a handful to a battalion, depending on the ships themselves.

The humans frequent integrated bionics, in particular, there is an odd trend for limb modification. Extra pairs of arms/forearms, additional legs resulting in a skittering gate, or small sublimbs that can grasp or manipulate interfaces.

Mage-Hunters are automatons that resemble slivers, albiet far more ornate and with an extra arm. They were designed for the war to seek out mana flows and to kill the mages who caused them, though now, they have been retrofitted to avoid such signatures and to rather go towards the radiation signatures indicative of moxite. There are horror stories told by some spire communities about Mage-Hunters coming in and ruthlessly hunting them down for enigmatic reason. Uniquely taking the place that slivers usually take in such grim children's tales.

The ships tend to range from small fighter-like crafts to troop carriers to battleships. Though, time and the environment have not been kind to these ships, most of them now lack much if any functional weaponry.

These ships will often send either the mage-hunters or themselves to the spire; either to mine moxite to keep their ships aloft or to trade with the moderates for the resources, as of the past three crashes, due to the expansion of the innards of the spire, moxite supplies have become more and more rare, which has resulted in the edgebound humans becoming slightly more aggressive and ruthless in recent memory.

Versebound Humans

The Versebound humans of the realm live in large ships, like floating stone that has been carved out to resemble fortresses or castles.

Human magics have degraded and are now relatively simple, simple battle magic, simple clay animation spells, simple spells to shape materials, simple divination spells.

Most of their tasks are not handled via magic, no, they are handled via animated Clay golems and entities known as 'The Directors'. The Golems manage physical tasks while the Director handles arcane, diplomatic, and societal tasks. The Directors are undead overseers of the sanctums they hold near dominion over. They keep the people alive, they use their corpses to accumulate mana to expand themselves or their sanctuaries, as well as to maintain them.

Some directives have gone rather extreme, to a degree that the humans within their sanctums are unaware of the fact that there even is an outside. While others will periodically send out humans periodically to the spire for etherium or as evangelists seeking immigrants.

Vampires

'Standard', 'Run of the mill', 'low level' Vampires are not able to sire Vampires themselves, nor do they have wings. Though their blood is a glowing golden light. Though after enough time has passed and enough blood has been drunk, they may metamorphose into the more angelic counterpart.

The 'Elder' Vampires of Hytropo are oddly angelic. They possess feathered bird-like wings that sprouts from their backs and allows them to fly. They protect the humans under their care within the spire, as rulers of cities within the caverns.

Unfortunately, they do not protect from a sense of altruism, they protect a food source. A food source which clans of Vampires will gladly go to war over. These conflicts are often painted as holy wars from which the Vampires 'must go to fight to rescue other poor humans from their oppressors'.

Certain cities maintain different degrees of relationships with outside ships, some are purely and economic trade, some are hostile, some are cordial peace, etc. Each city's vampire lord, however, will always try to maintain a positive relationship with Homebound Humans who come to them, after all, they have a sense for when and how the spire will change, and knowing that sort of information can mean the difference between the life and death of a city.

Some cities have overthrown their Vampire overlords, and, as such, are known as 'Free Cities'. These Free Cities are often assaulted and besieged by Vampires who use various different arguments or justifications to get human militias to join them.

Homebound Humans

Their flesh is a dark metal, which they claim itches when near moxite, and their eyes are a silvery metal, which their vision goes blurry when they near etherium.

They tend to live away from societies, travelling and exploring the spire, slaying slivers, and mining the precious metals and gems within heart of the structure.

Beasts

Hytropo has two unique fauna to the realm.

The first is a mischievous shadow fiend. It enjoys getting into locked boxes and rooms and taking random bits and bobs from within that it can find. It will spend months learning how to pick a stubborn lock or trying to figure out the keycode for a door, only to steal a lock or dice. Though, as more and more items disappear, they begin to take more and more important items, the spigot handle for the ship's drive, a few chunks of etherium, a gun from the turret mount, supposedly, even people from their beds. Getting rid of them is a matter of dissuading them, laxing security or increasing it to the degree that it dies in it's attempts to infiltrate. The creatures make nests within the covered craters of the ancient battlefields or hidden cracks and crevices within the spire.

The next is technically a flora, touched with eldrazi influence is what scholars who believe in alternate realities assume. It is a patch of color, it can be near any color, but not whatever color the object is supposed to be, usually inverted but sometimes it is as simple as extremely brighter or darker. This, technically moss, breaks down the mana and aether of the object that houses it, fueling truly explosive growth. A city within the spire that discovers such an infestation may have their tunnels collapsed, ships fired upon, quarters within sanctums incinerated. The Slivers of the realm produce a 'natural pesticide' that can slow down or even clear up infestations, but that requires an extensive preparation period that very few societies of the realm have taken to preparing.

Dragons

The dragon of Hytropo, Savomatrix, is a long serpentine creature with only one pair of limbs, the ends of which are hand-like appendages connected to wings. The fins on the serpentine tail is akin to the fins of a tadpole. He swims through the fog that remains from the ancient war and he keeps to himself, having only cursory interactions with Humans if he can help it.

He has the ability to displace large swaths of beings via space-time, only to resurface in another time and another place upon the realm, sometimes appearing within the caverns of the spire crashes later, sometimes out in the middle of nowhere moments later.

Esoterica

The forms of Esoterica of Hytropo are comprised of The Dawning Esoterica, The Esoterica Warform, The Esoterica Historica, and The Esoterica Reality Render.

The Dawning Esoterica is the same as in each realm. Roughly eight feet tall, far stronger than their size would suggest, capable of magic from ages past. Unique in their status amongst the Esoterica of the realm as their leader or perhaps even as a parent depending on the Esoterica.

The Esoterica Warforms are in few number, but they are mighty. Converge is 3. They were, like all of the realm's Warforms, all but wiped out during the March of the Machines.

The Esoterica Historica are in few number, but they are wise. Converge is 3. They were instrumental in the defense of Hytropo, bestowing the lost knowledge of magics and artilleries to the Humans to aide in the defense of the realm. This knowledge has resulted in the cold war between the Edgebound and Versebound humans becoming escalated and tense compared to before.

The Esoterica Reality Render are in few number, but they are horrific. Converge is 3. By far the physically weakest variety of Esoterica on Hytropo, but, they have the ability simply delete something from reality every crash or to make knowledge not currently in use to disappear into the annuls of history. They were responsible for the loss of the super weapons from the Humans of the realm, but the Historica have undone some of the work they have spent centuries on.
