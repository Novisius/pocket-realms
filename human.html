Human

"The old stories claim that our ancestors conquered worlds, they bled a star to vanquish the dark, they fought that which extinguished the light. We served gods who some believe abandoned us...what is it that you believe?" - Unknown

Humans were once Auxiliary forces for the Fomori in their multiple wars. Notably serving as Foreign Legions against the Eldrazi of the Exoverse, and as Penal Legions against the Vampires of Ixalan. Though, due to time and the Cataclysms, their once rigid militarism has given way to multiple different cultures, shaped by the mana of their home. They have, generally, retained working amounts of Exosuits, Managuns, Lightblades, knowledge of Cosmium infusions onto their own persons, as well as War Machines and vehicles of old. For some reason, those affected by the Cataclyms do not usually seek to use, maintain, or make these artifacts for themselves.

Generally speaking, Humans are equivalent to an unadapted Slivers in strength, durability, and speed. (1/1s)

Due to the Cataclysms, there are several 'aberrant' populations that seem to rely on their altered capacities rather than the ones they were left by the Fomori. Not that they don't acknowledge them as useful, but that they seem to have a strange aversion to the technologies. Not to say that they won't use them, but more that they have a preference to simply not.

The Lightless

Beings born from the first cataclysm and improved by the second, they are near humans, their forms monochrome, their blood gone, their bodies cold, their hearts replaced with magical singularities (the photon collars the same colors of their realm), and they became biologically immortal. They can see the vitality of others as something they describe to be a 'spark of light', which they can not see in one another, hence they have given themselves the moniker of 'lightless'. An odd fact or feature of these beings is that their fertility rates are truly abysmal. When they first came into being, they had a fertility rate of ~.815 children per female (within a period of a normal Human lifespan and a normal fertility rate of 1.63), however, the second cataclysm worsened the issue, reducing the rate to ~.271. Due to their unageing nature, and low birth rates, a majority of the Lightless who exist today existed during the time of the first cataclysm.

They have the ability to teleport around their homerealm, sort of consuming themselves within their own singularity, until the singularity vanishes, reappearing somewhere else with a small burst of wind and a pop. (At the beginning of your upkeep, you may exile this creature, if you do, return it to the battlefield under it's owner's control.)

The souls of those who die near them (within a handful of meters) are inexorably consumed and shredded by the singularity within their chests. A portion of the vitality given to the Lightless, while a portion of it is expelled as minorly harmful levels of radiation via their photon collars. (Whenever this creature or another creature or artifact you control dies, target opponent gains 1 rad counter and you gain 1 life.)

Those who are willing can consciously choose to feed themselves to the singularity of a Lightless, their matter enhancing and reinforcing the matter of the Lightless. This process has granted a number of lightless strength and durability beyond the imaginations of us mere mortals. However, this strengthening has an odd quirk, it disappears when they teleport, which has made them view the convenient transportation as a last resort, something to save their lives rather than as a preferred travel method. (Whenever you sacrifice another creature or artifact, put a +1/+1 counter on this creature.)

The Perpetuals

Beings born from the third cataclysm, their hair became a dust-like material akin to a nebulae and their irises became starfields.

These people are the only ones who survive death upon the realms. When they die, their souls shed their mortal flesh and becomes something immaterial, yet solidly real. Ghosts and Echoes of what they once were. (When this creature dies, exile it and create a token copy of it, except the token is a spirit instead of a human, it has a base power and toughness of 1/1, and it gains absorb 1.) They are stripped of most emotions and drives, leaving only the last or strongest emotion or drive they had in life. Able to pursue their newfound purpose eternally, as they lose the need to eat, drink, and sleep. Over time, some can overcome the scars of death, regranting them a mind similar to what they possessed in life.

Dissipating one of these echoes is a task few try to undertake, not only because it is difficult, but because they retain their knowledge and memories of life, making them windows to ages past, some of which may have little to no written records.

The Sundrous

Beings born from the fourth cataclysm, they have strange shadows, both shadows that go away from and towards sources of light.

They can have these shadows animate into physical bodies that look and act just as substantial as the Sundrous who spawned them, looking near identical to the individual, changing appearance based on actions the Sundrous may have taken earlier in the day, that would still lead to this moment. They, and the Sundrous, each lack all shadows. The Sundrous and their 'others' each take actions the Sundrous was likely to take simultaneously, before disappearing shortly afterwards. (Myriad) The others are autonomous, reacting to stimulus and situations like the Sundrous would. A small fraction of the memories going to the Sundrous, about ten minutes worth, divided amongst every Other.

The Visionaries

Beings born from the fifth cataclysm, their eyes glow and reflect light, objects, and scenes not present.

They possess the ability to see into the future as shadows, the distance and accuracy of these visions can be increased with effort and training, though there is no insignificant amount of skill involved in the process. ( one to five {X}: Scry X or Fateseal X.)

Those who do not train can be plagued with improbable visions of the world seconds in advance from it not happening, which can cause what is perceived as schizophrenic symptoms. Such individuals are best served by being forced to train their control over their ability.

The most common refinement of this ability is narrowing down the shadows they see to those of likely futures or hours ahead, refining further to most likely futures or weeks ahead, and finally refining with the most skilled Visionaries to seeing the future that will occur rather than what could occur or seeing years ahead. There is typically a trade off between accuracy (likelihood) and depth (distance from the present) that these shadows display as.

The Werewolves

Beings born from the seventh cataclysm, they grew horns and antlers, eyes like a cat's, their hair color changed to a color or colors related to their home realm's color identity, slightly pointed ears, and they grew tusks not unlike an elephant's in form.

While in daylight, be it direct or soft, they appear more or less human, with the above changes. Though they are more innately magical and physically inclined. (2/2s. Creatures have protection from every creature not in their sector until the end of combat. If multiple creatures have the Phased keyword, divide the battlefield into a number of sectors equal to the highest number of Phased.), Flanking, Enrage - Deal 2 damage to any target, and +1/+1. Human.) Realms with equitorial bands of desert have populations of perpetual dayformed Werewolves.

While in night light, the subtle shimmer of the exosphere, they become wolf-like monstrosities. They become larger, their horns and tusks grow out, they become covered in fur, they grow claws, fangs, and the like. In short, they become werewolves with certain eccentricities. Their personalities shift to becoming far more violent, aggressive, and hungry. They socialize with one another, but not with any other variety of Human, Splinter, or other similar being. Their memories, cognition, and personalities are entirely divorced from their daylight and eclipsed forms. (8/8s. Annihilator 4, Absorb 4, Phased 8, 'Flanking, Flanking, Flanking, Flanking', Enrage - Deal 8 damage to any target, and +4/+4. Human creature type replaced by Werewolf.) Realms with arctic poles have populations of perpetual nightformed Werewolves.

While deprived of or in the presence of both daylight and nightlight, they become pitiful creatures. They look emaciated, their horns shrink and grow becoming asymmetric and gangly, their tusks shrink to little more than slightly longer canines, their upper canines grow out to match the length of their lower canines, their eyes become a normal Human's, their hair loses their luster, and they lose their innate arcane abilities. Psychologically, they become erratic, isolationist, and generally paranoid, specifically that everyone is trying to kill them and that they will die in the sunlight and nightlight. Unlike the dayform and nightform, they lack consistent contextual memories, as this form usually does not occur for long if at all in the Werewolf's life, at best appearing briefly during dawn and dusk, only to 'cease' as the day rises or the night falls. They grow claws and their ears change to something wolf-like. (1/2 / 1/2s. Human Werewolves.) In the realms of the 0th, 1st, and 5th strata, there are populations of perpetual eclipseformed werewolves who live under the surface of the central world.
