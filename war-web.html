War Web

During the Fomori-Eldrazi War, the Fomori of the Multiverse travelled across the chaos wall frequently. To facilitate the supply lines for this war, they built staging grounds into the chaos wall, spaces between the finite infinity between the multiverse and the edge.

These pockets stabilized the chaos, concentrated it into mana, and then concentrated it into matter. A self perpetuating Engine of expansion that served as staging grounds, relay stations, and recharging stations. Each one aligned with different colors of mana to serve as perfect 'batteries' for different machines and purposes.

The Fomori had many war machines, but with the war against the Eldrazi and Ixalan both going simultaneously, they implemented an unusual force to safeguard these locations from the near constant invasions of the Eldrazi. They used a peculiar organism they found within both the edge and the multiverse, Slivers.

Making artifacts to control their hiveminds, they were able to have the Slivers reliably safeguard the planar portals and eternity columns of the artificial worlds from their bastion moons, allowing themselves, their ships, and their war machines to pass through uncontested, while aggressively fighting and adapting to the Eldrazi menace. A strategy not unlike the methods employed by Volrath to guard the Legacy, but on a more grand scale.

The Fomori did not come into these pockets entirely devoid of other forms of life. Bringing in the slivers and coming in themselves, they had various hitchhikers, ancestors of the flora and fauna that can be found in the pockets today. Though, as they were not intentionally seeded with life nor designed to host life other than Slivers, major forms of life are few and not particularly diverse.

Since the Fomori disappeared, the pockets expanded, life continued to adapt, and the concentrations of mana warped the landscape. As they expanded, they weakened the wall in specific nebulous points, which has facilitated the crossing between the two occasionally. Such as Tezzeret entering into the Edge.

Various unintended side effects of these pockets have become prominent over the millennia, leylines from the Edge slowly grows Moxite along them, while leylines from the Multiverse slowly grows Etherium along them. They act as a sort of 'self-repairing' 'natural' wire that conducts the mana into the central 'engine'. The Eastern Pole is predominantly where the multiversal leylines come from, while the Western Pole is predominantly where the Exoversal leylines come from. Overtime, the denizens of the realms have mined and harvested the materials, which has resulted in a higher concentration in the center of the realms, where the leylines converge and feed into the 'engine'.

The souls of the Slivers, along with the various modifications they made to their biology, were designed to use the leylines to feed into the 'engine'. The dead have their souls shredded, mere remnants at best remain, while the rest travels along the leylines. The edges, emotions, memories, non-sliver form/identity, all snag along the leylines, forming and growing cosmium deposits along them. Unlike the veins/wires of metal that grow from the outside inwards, these veins grow from the inside outwards.

Due to the constant invasions of Eldrazi and the hyper territorial Slivers, major settlements have never appeared on any of the realms outside of their home bastion moon. There are small pockets of society on many of the realm's main worlds, but that is just about all there is in nations.

These realms were staging grounds for military forces, those forces were Human auxilaries, foreign legions, and/or penal legions. Outfitted with exosuits fueled by moxite, mana guns and light blades made of etherium, and various modifications from cosmium infusions. They were left here when the Fomori disappeared, and it wasn't long before the slivers began to view them as threats and began to attack them. Those that remain have maintained only a shadow of their past glories, with Cataclysms, major catastrophes from the multiverse and exoverse, having modified random fractions of their populations, creating further distinct sub populations of Human.

The most recent cataclysm has resulted in the arrival of Splinters, beings who had evolved independence in a version of the realms where humans were never left behind.

Oddly, Eldrazi incursions have become less frequent in the past decade or so.

General Ecology

The central planet of each realm rotates. A full rotation lasts twenty five hours exactly, regardless of the size of the world, each day and night lasting six hours and fifteen minutes exactly, having a total of two of both per rotation. When the worlds face the multiverse or exoverse, it is day, while they face the chaos wall, it is night. The planes and star systems of the multiverse and exoverse respectively form stars that get filtered through the exosphere of the realm into sunlight.

Due to this rotation, they have a warmer equator and colder poles, their poles are trapped in perpetual night while the equators are trapped in near perpetual daylight.

On the horizon, beyond the skies, through the first two layers of the Exospheres lies a basic translation of the blind eternities into something real and definable. Shadows dance across the skies through this translation, some quick, others languid, but all are the forms of Eldrazi within the Chaos Wall. These shadows are best analogous in appearance to dark clouds, higher than clouds should be, capable of moving behind the stars.

Often, these shadows will move into the exosphere and result in an eldrazi drone or two. This occurs frequently enough that the realmic denizens are unaware of the fact that Eldrazi are not normal forms of fauna.

The rest of the life on the realms are evolutions of pests, microbial life, and fungal growths.

Hive Bastions

Orbitting each world, within their atmospheres, are small moons. Some have many, some have few. Generated and expanded by the engines when particular evolutionary triggers are met, resulting in a great variance between the number of bastion moons around any given realm, even amongst the same strata.

Within the center of each bastion is a liquid metal sphere, a structure made to sustain that particular hive's population, spawning a continual stream of thrum, spawning quicker the larger these cores are.

Human Bastion

Slowly expanded, one of the bastion moons was designed to house a human garrison, in the event that the realms were invaded and the slivers fell. Recently, they have been expanded to house those who are displaced by Sliver aggression.

Exosphere

The Exosphere is composed of three distinct spheres.

The first sphere, the Glimmervoid, like a fog of silvery dust, translates mana into matter and accumulates it into the realm. It also serves to recycle matter that enters into it as material deposited into the realms.

The second sphere, the Mana Confluence, is nothing but light, heat, mana, and radiation. This sphere translates the aether from the blind eternities into mana and energy.

The third and final sphere, the Echoes of Eternity, a shadowy scape that can drive those who look upon it mad. Shadows of the true forms of eldrazi move within, glimpses of the exosphere and multiverse can be seen through it, and the destructive aether that annihilates matter infusing it all. This sphere somehow translating the blind eternities of the chaos wall into a definable tangible reality.

The more colors of mana that needs to be processed or manufactured, the less stable the process is and the larger the exosphere needs to be.
