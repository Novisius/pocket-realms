War Web

During the Fomori-Eldrazi War, the Fomori of the Multiverse travelled across the chaos wall frequently. To facilitate the supply lines for this war, they built staging grounds into the chaos wall, spaces between the finite infinity between the multiverse and the edge.

These pockets stabilized the chaos, concentrated it into mana, and then concentrated it into matter. A self perpetuating Engine of expansion that served as staging grounds, relay stations, and recharging stations. Each one aligned with different colors of mana to serve as perfect 'batteries' for different machines and purposes.

The Fomori had many war machines, but with the war against the Eldrazi and Ixalan both going simultaneously, they implemented an unusual force to safeguard these locations from the near constant invasions of the Eldrazi. They used a peculiar organism they found within both the edge and the multiverse, Slivers.

Making artifacts to control their hiveminds, they were able to have the Slivers reliably safeguard the planar portals and eternity columns that were built into the center of these pockets, allowing themselves, their ships, and their war machines to pass through uncontested, while aggressively fighting and adapting to the Eldrazi menace. A strategy not unlike the methods employed by Volrath to guard the Legacy, but on a more grand scale.

The Fomori did not come into these pockets entirely devoid of other forms of life. Bringing in the slivers and coming in themselves, they had various hitchhikers, ancestors of the flora and fauna that can be found in the pockets today. Though, as they were not intentionally seeded with life nor designed to host life other than Slivers, major forms of life are few and not particularly diverse.

Since the Fomori disappeared, the pockets expanded, life continued to adapt, and the concentrations of mana warped the landscape. As they expanded, they weakened the wall in specific nebulous points, which has facilitated the crossing between the two occasionally. Such as Tezzeret entering into the Edge.

Various unintended side effects of these pockets have become prominent over the millennia, leylines from the Edge slowly grows Moxite along them, while leylines from the Multiverse slowly grows Etherium along them. They act as a sort of 'self-repairing' 'natural' wire that conducts the mana into the central 'engine'. Overtime, the denizens of the realms have mined and harvested the materials, which has resulted in a higher concentration in the center of the realms, where the leylines converge and feed into the 'engine'.

The souls of the Slivers, along with the various modifications they made to their biology, were designed to use the leylines to feed into the 'engine'. The souls of everything else, was not. The dead have their souls shredded, mere remnants at best remain, while the rest travels along the leylines. The edges, emotions, memories, non-sliver form/identity, all snag along the leylines, forming and growing crystals of mana along them. Unlike the veins/wires of metal that grow from the outside inwards, these crystals grow from the inside outwards.

Due to the constant invasions of Eldrazi and the hyper territorial Slivers, major settlements have never appeared on any of the realms. There are small pockets of society on many of the realms, but that is just about all there is in nations.

Every realm has independently developed their own forms of sentient life, each though, is an odd mirror of one another, perhaps due to the innate interconnectivity each of the realms possess through the 'engine's connected planar portals and eternity columns. Every realm has beastial animalfolk known simply as the 'Kin' and Aetherborn, who are born from the quelling of the blind eternities around the realms. Some realms have Echoes, gestalt souls of the dead; Remnants, machines left by the Fomori; and/or Splinters, individualist descended from the Sliver hives.

Oddly, Eldrazi incursions have become less frequent in the past decade or so. Similarly, Sliver attacks against Remnants travelling through the Hivelands have become more frequent.

Despite each realm being objectively flat, a half-sphere of land and atmosphere, there is a visual illusion, perhaps induced by magic, perhaps some sort of gravitational distortion, that causes the realm to visually curve at roughly a rate of 0.009 degrees per kilometer to those on them.
